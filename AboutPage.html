What is Maternal Mental Health?

Maternal mental health refers to the mental and emotional well-being of women during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period.
It encompasses a range of psychological conditions, from common mood changes to more serious mental health disorders.

Why is it Important?

Addressing maternal mental health is essential not only for the well-being of the mother but also for the baby’s development and the family’s overall health.
Early identification and intervention are crucial, as untreated mental health issues can have long-term consequences.
This is why it's important to raise awareness about maternal mental health, reduce stigma, and encourage open conversations about the emotional challenges of pregnancy and motherhood.

If you or someone you know is struggling with maternal mental health, it’s important to seek help from a healthcare professional to get the support needed.
Our website was made for easy access and general information, consulting a professional in person is always the better option.
